Welcome to my case study for Louis Dreyfus.

In this case study, I attempt to answer 3 questions:

  • How much iron ore did Brazil export in May 2015?
  • Did the ships that left the iron ore ports in May week 4 come from (1) South Africa / the Indian Ocean, or (2) North Atlantic Ocean?
  • How do we explain the price movements in the daily hire file based on the position history?

Data Sets

These are the data sets which will assist me in answering these questions. I have converted them to CSV for ease of reading in R.

  • Fleet register, which identifies ships (name, deadweight = carrying capacity, age etc.) by their unique IMO (International Maritime Organization) number.
  • Portlog, which specifies the geographical coordinates (latitude minmax, longitude minmax) of areas containing some of the major world ports.
  • Position data for all of 2015-to-date, with daily position history for the entire fleet. This is something called AIS data.
  • Daily hire rate for which Capesize vessels (deadweight > 120,000) got paid ($/day) when embarking on a new round-trip voyage from a given discharge port, to a load port, and back to a discharge port.

How do we explain the price movements in the daily hire file based on the position history?

Generally, the hire rate of the vessels (supply) increases as the demand for iron ore increases. The demand for iron ore can be inferred from the amount of iron ore exported.
